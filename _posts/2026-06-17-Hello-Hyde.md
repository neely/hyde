If you're reading this, you just forked Hyde and your blog is live.

This is the one sample post that ships with the template, mostly so you have something to look at and something to delete. Feel free to edit it, replace it, or remove it entirely once you've found your footing.

A few things worth knowing before you write your first real post:

**This blog is a Jekyll site hosted on GitHub Pages.** Every post is a Markdown file in `_posts/`, named `YYYY-MM-DD-Your-Title.md`. The date controls sort order; the title becomes your URL.

**You can write from anywhere using Hyde's composer.** Open `/compose.html` on this site — yes, right now, in another tab — and you'll find a mobile-friendly writing tool that commits directly to this repo using the GitHub API. No server, no app, no account beyond a GitHub Personal Access Token.

**Drafts are supported.** Toggle "Save as draft" in the composer and your post goes to `_drafts/` instead of `_posts/`, invisible on the live site until you publish it for real.

**Images work by pasting.** Screenshot something, paste it into the body while writing, and it gets staged, resized, and committed to `/images/` automatically.

Once you're comfortable, head back to your repo's `_config.yml` and fill in your real name, bio, and social links — the placeholders are there to make it obvious what needs changing.

Welcome to Hyde. Go write something.
